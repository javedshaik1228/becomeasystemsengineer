# Mission: C++ systems and networking technical rounds

## Why
Become ready to perform strongly in technical rounds for C++ systems, networking,
storage, and security roles like the supplied Cisco, NetApp, HPE, Mastercard,
and low-latency engineering openings.

## Success looks like
- Build, test, debug, profile, and explain a non-trivial C++20 network service on Linux/WSL or macOS.
- Reason precisely about lifetime, ownership, undefined behavior, concurrency, syscalls, TCP, IPC, storage, and failure modes.
- Solve coding and debugging exercises aloud while making defensible trade-offs.
- Lead a 45-minute system-design discussion and connect choices to role-specific networking or storage concerns.
- Complete two scored mock loops at 80% or better with no critical safety gap.

## Constraints
- Fourteen days; approximately 40 focused hours.
- Project-based, retrieval-heavy practice with automatic feedback where possible.
- Linux/WSL is the primary runtime; macOS-compatible paths are included.
- The repository must remain usable without invoking the Teach plugin again.

## Out of scope
- Claiming production mastery of SMB/NFS internals, ASIC SDKs, routing protocols, cryptography, or distributed storage in two weeks.
- LeetCode-style breadth unrelated to systems and networking interviews.
- Windows-native socket programming; Windows users work inside WSL.
