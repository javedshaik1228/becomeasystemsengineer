# Senior-signal story bank

The senior roles ask for ownership, ambiguity, cross-team influence, mentoring,
root-cause analysis, and design judgment. Prepare evidence; do not invent scale
or responsibility. Each story gets a 90-second version and a five-minute deep dive.

## Story template

- **Context (15 sec):** system, customer impact, your actual scope.
- **Constraint (20 sec):** ambiguity, failure, deadline, or competing priorities.
- **Decision (30 sec):** options considered, evidence gathered, trade-off chosen.
- **Execution (45 sec):** what you personally did and how others were involved.
- **Result (20 sec):** measured outcome; name uncertainty honestly.
- **Reflection (20 sec):** what you would repeat or change now.

## Prepare these six stories

1. A cross-layer production defect you reduced from symptom to root cause.
2. A resource, race, latency, or reliability issue you measured before fixing.
3. An ambiguous feature you turned into requirements, design, and delivery.
4. A technical disagreement where you changed or aligned the decision with evidence.
5. A code/design review or mentoring moment that improved another engineer's work.
6. A failure or wrong hypothesis and the mechanism you changed afterward.

## Probe sheet

For every story, answer: What was the invariant? What competing hypothesis did you
rule out? What metric moved? What did you own versus the team? What failure mode
remained? How did rollout/recovery work? What would break at 10× load? Which log,
trace, packet, stack, or profile was decisive?

If a story cannot survive those probes, narrow the claim until it can.
