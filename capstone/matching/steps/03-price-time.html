Recall before reading

Which bid and ask are best? State both crossing inequalities. Why must orders at one price remain FIFO?

Mental model

A buy crosses while best ask ≤ its limit; a sell crosses while best bid ≥ its limit. The taker pays the resting maker’s price. Every fill must conserve quantity: executed plus remaining equals the incoming quantity.

Build

  1. Use a queue-like sequence inside each price level.
  2. Match only the best opposing level first.
  3. Handle partial maker and taker quantities.
  4. Continue across levels until filled or the limit no longer crosses.
  5. Erase empty makers and empty price levels immediately.

Break it deliberately

Use two same-price makers, a taker larger than both, and a second worse price. Reverse sides. Confirm the fill stream is deterministic and maker IDs remain in arrival order.

cmake -S capstone/matching/work -B build/matching-work -DMATCHING_STEP=3
cmake --build build/matching-work -j
ctest --test-dir build/matching-work --output-on-failure
Hint 1 · loop shape

Each iteration inspects the current best level, fills its oldest maker, then decides whether the level or loop is finished.

Hint 2 · symmetry

Keep buy and sell branches visually parallel until both are correct; abstract only after tests prove symmetry.

Stuck protocol. At 20 minutes, simulate one partial fill on paper. At 40, assert quantity conservation after each fill. At 60, compare one matching branch in the reference, close it, and write the opposite branch yourself.
